This is a must-read for anyone starting a new leadership role, be it for the first time or after a long tenure. It walks through the many pitfalls there are (of which I've fallen into many myself) and provides a solid framework for navigating the critical initial time in a new position. The advice and how it was structured made it easy to digest and apply — almost to the point that I felt like applying for a new role just to put it into practice!

- I wish I had read this book earlier in my career; it would have saved me from many common pitfalls as a fledling leader.

- I loved how mindful the book is about the many different aspects of a leadership and how it lays out a clear roadmap for balancing them, from building relationships to setting strategy, from your direct reports and theirs in turn to peers and superiors.

- The one thing I missed was pointers for better learning organizational design. The book kept pointing this out as a critical skill and common gap in many leaders, but I would have loved more concrete advice on how to get better at this, or resources to explore.

- My main takeaway was the ten skills presented in the book, which constituted the backbone of the framework for success in the first 90 days: preparing yourself, accelerating your learning, matching strategy to situation, securing early wins, negotiating success, achieving alignment, building your team, creating coalitions, keeping your balance, and accelerating everyone.

- It's easy to bring with you habits from previous roles that don't fit the new context, and this book does a great job of highlighting that risk and providing tools to avoid it.

- Relationships are key to success in a new role, and the book emphasizes the importance of quickly building trust and rapport, not only vertically with your team and superiors but also horizontally with peers and other stakeholders.

- The book has a lot of checklists and tables, which just in themselves are worth the read.

- The STARS framework (start-up, turnaround, accelerated growth, realignment, sustaining success) for diagnosing business situations added some nice nuance to the advice on strategy and approach.

- The different types of decisions-making processes (consult-and-decide, build consensus) helped me reflect on how I approach decisions in my own leadership, and to do so more consciously.

- Overall, just like the book Manager's Path, this book brings to the surface different styles of leadership and management, helping you reflect on your own approach and how to adapt it to different situations, as well as pushing you to grow in areas you might not have considered before.

- There are A LOT of different lists and tables. Some of them are great resources but many felt somewhat obsolete — not all ideas require a checklist to implement.
