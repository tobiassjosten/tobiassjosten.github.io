Meditations is one of the most impactful books I've ever read. It ignited something in me that has kept growing stronger since that fateful day I picked up the book. I'm happier, more focused, more resilient, and more at peace because of the lessons I've learned from Marcus Aurelius.

I couldn't recommend this book enough. In fact, it's the #1 book in my list of mandatory reading for my children, and the only book I keep re-reading every few years to refresh my mind on its teachings.

- One of the biggest takeaways for me, and something that has had a profound influence on me since reading the book is this central tenant: We are not bothered by events, but by how we interpret them.

- I love the humility of Aurelius — the emperor of one of the greatest nations in history — and how he struggles with the most common human issues: anger, frustration, anxiety, impatience, pride, envy, lust, greed. He is constantly reminding himself to be humble, to accept what he cannot change, to focus on what is within his control.

- It would be easy to misinterpret Stoicism as a philosophy of passivity, given how often Aurelius reminds himself to accept what is outside of his control. But that is not the case at all. Stoicism is about focusing your energy on what you can control, and taking action there. It is about being proactive in your own life, rather than reactive to external events.

- The extreme focus on living by the virtues of wisdom, justice, courage, and temperance as the only true goodness life is also something that has stuck with me. In a world where we are constantly bombarded with distractions and temptations, it is easy to lose sight of what is truly important, and to view a righteous life as an unobtainable ideal. Aurelius reminds us that, with each daily action, however small and seemingly insignificant, we can choose to live virtuously.
