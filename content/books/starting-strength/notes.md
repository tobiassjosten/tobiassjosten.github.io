 Coming from the CrossFit world, I was initially skeptical of Mark Rippetoe's Starting Strength program. However, seeing others getting impressive results with it piqued my interest. After following the program for several months, I can confidently say that it was one of the best decisions I've made for my fitness journey. One of those decisions that you only regret not making sooner.

 Having coached hundreds of people through the program, I've seen firsthand how effective it is for building strength and muscle. If that is your goal, look no further. It's simple, straightforward, and it works. Just don't confuse simple with easy — the program is challenging, but the results are worth it.

I wrote an in-depth guide for getting started with and running the program at Athlegan: https://athlegan.com/starting-strength

- Rippetoe is extremely dogmatic, there is no "maybe" in his book. This can be off-putting to some, but I found it refreshing. He has a clear vision of what works and what doesn't, and he sticks to it. Above all, the results back it up. One huge caveat to this is that Rippetoe also likes to throw around advice about areas that are outside his expertise, such as nutrition and politics. This should be taken with a grain of salt.

- Starting Strength prescribes true functional training and was conceived way before the term became popular and misinterpret by online fitness clowns.

- While the book is aimed at novices, this term is defined differently from how most gymgoers would interpret it. Simply put, unless you feel confident in the barbell squat and can comfortably deadlift twice your bodyweight, you are a novice. This means that even "intermediate" lifters can benefit from the book.
